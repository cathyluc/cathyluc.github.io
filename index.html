The Lakes


Guide to the Lakes is a guidebook composed of Romantic poetries about the Lakes District in England written by William Wordsworth. It is frequently studied by scholars as an early influence on the 19th-century geography.

A study from Lancaster University investigated this corpus that comprises 80 works written between 1622 and 1900. It demonstrates how a hybrid geographical and corpus-based methodology can be used to study historical writing about the Lakeland region.
